<h>Election night: how it unfolded

<p>Matthew Tempest and Richard Moriarty
Friday June 8, 2001Guardian
 Unlimited

<p>
&#149; Blair walks back into No 10 Downing Street

<p>&#149; 5.50am
 Mr Blair praises Neil Kinnock as the man who started
 the modernisation of Labour party and tells flag-waving supporters
 "This is a night of history for our party. 

<p>"The one thing we have to remember is that now is the time when
 the people of this country want us to serve them, and want us
 to do the things we promised to do. "Our mandate is to carry
 on the work that we started." 

<p> &#149; 5.50am
Tony Blair greets staff and supporters at Labour
 HQ, Millbank Tower.

<p>
&#149; 5.45am
Tory supporting comedian Jim Davidson arrives
 at Conservative central office saying: "I'm just scratching
 my head thinking 'am I part of this country now?' Everyone else
 has voted for Tony Blair - I must be absolutely out of touch."

<p>
&#149; 5.20am
 Lib Dems hold Winchester
&#149; Tories fail to
 win Monmouth, leaving them with no seats in Wales again.

<p>&#149; 5.15am
The prime minister's plane touches down in London.

<p>
&#149; 5.07am
Romsey Sandra Gidley holds the seat the Lib Dems
 won at a byelection last year.

<p>
&#149; 5.01am
 Lib Dem Jackie Ballard loses her Taunton seat
 to the Conservatives.

<p>
&#149; 4.32am
Ken Clarke holds his Rushcliffe seat.

<p>&#149; 4.29amRichmond Conservative leader William Hague keeps
 his seat, increasing the majority by more than 6,000. He says:
 "Results are deeply disappointing for my party. We must respect
 the verdict of the voters and listen to what they have said.
 It must be a sobering lesson for all parties that millions have
 not taken part in this election. I will set out my views for
 how we revive the party later this morning."

<p>&#149; 4.25amDevizes Conservative party chairman Michael Ancram
 holds his seat.

<p>
&#149; 4.15amDorset South Labour wins its top target seat, its
 second gain of the night.

<p>
 &#149; 4.02amHenley Boris Johnson takes responsibility for
 true-blue seat from Michael Heseltine, quoting Bob Dylan: "They
 say the darkest hour comes just before the dawn."

<p>
&#149; 3.50am
Tory party strategist and speechwriter Danny Finkelstein
 fails to regain Harrow West.

<p>
&#149; 3.45amFolkestone & Hythe Michael Howard holds his seat.

<p>
&#149; 3.42am
Hague leaves constituency home for count in Richmond.

<p>
&#149; 3.40am
William Hague concedes defeat in private telephone
 call to Tony Blair, and congratulates him on victory.

<p>
 &#149; 2.10am Charles Kennedy easily holds Ross, Skye and Inverness
 West, majority rising from 4,000 to 13,000.
Tories gain Tatton.

<p>
 &#149; 2.03am
Tories take their first seat from Labour by winning
 Romford with a swing of 9.2%.

<p>&#149; 2.02am
Lib Dems take Mid Dorset off the Tories.

<p>&#149; 1.59am
 Michael Portillo holds Kensington & Chelsea -
 with 10% drop in turnout.

<p>
&#149; 1.56am
Lib Dems increase majority in their second most
 vulnerable seat, Kingston & Surbiton, from 56 votes to nearly
 16,000.

<p>
&#149; 1.26amHartlepool Peter Mandelson holds his seat: "I am
 a fighter, not a quitter." Arthur Scargill polls 912 votes.

 &#149; The controversial former paymaster-general, Geoffrey
 Robinson, holds Coventry North East.

<p>
&#149; 1.35am
Recounts taking place in Chesterfield, Guildford,
 Cheadle and Perth.

<p>&#149; 1.34am
BBC poll now predicts a Labour majority of 173.

<p>&#149; 1.29am
Tony Blair holds Sedgefield - with a reduced majority
 and a swing to the Tories of 4.7%. Mr Blair claims national
 victory for Labour, saying the re-election of a Labour government
 would show the public "agree with the direction of this government
 but they want us to get on with the journey".

<p>&#149;1.23am
 Michael Meacher holds neighbouring Oldham West
 & Royton - but BNP leader Nick Griffin polls more than 6,000
 votes.

<p>&#149; 1.20am
Phil Woolas holds Oldham East and Saddleworth for
 Labour - but after race riots, BNP poll 5,091.

<p>
&#149; 1.16amSt Helens South Shaun Woodward holds his new seat
 - but is booed. Socialist Alliance polls more than 2,500 votes.

 &#149; Jordan fails to take Stretford and Urmston.

<p>
&#149; 1.08am
 Tories fail to recapture Eastwood - prior to
 1997, one of their safest seats in Scotland.

<p>
&#149; 1.03am
 Hotly tipped Estelle Morris holds her Birmingham
 seat - after earlier wobble.

<p>
&#149; 1am
Labour take Anglesey from Plaid Cymru.

<p>
&#149; 12.50am
 "It appears the Conservatives have not been
 successful tonight but it also appears we will make some gains,"
 admits Michael Portillo. Ken Clarke refuses to speculate on
 William Hague's future.

<p>&#149; 12.31am
Adrian Sanders, returned as the Lib Dem MP for
 Torbay, says he is "staggered, stunned, amazed" at the size
 of the 6,708 majority with which he won. 

<p>&#149; 12.30am
 Tony Blair arrives at the Sedgefield count.

<p>&#149; 12.25amTorbay Lib Dems hold - was Tory's No.2 target.
 Lib Dem majority up from 12, to more than 6,000. Southport also
 held by the Lib Dems.

<p>
&#149; 12.21am
 Deputy prime minister John Prescott holds Hull
 East, but turnout down 13%.

<p>
&#149; 12.05amDunfermline EastChancellor Gordon Brown holds
 his super-safe Labour seat, with turnout down 13%
 &#149; Clare
 Short holds Birmingham Ladywood.

<p>
&#149; 11.41pm
 Northern Ireland secretary John Reid condemns
 suspected republican shooting of two RUC officers in Draperstown,
 Co Londonderry; "They &#91;the culprits&#93; have no mandate
 and they dare not seek a mandate because what they represent
 has been rejected time and again by the people of Ireland, north
 and south. My thoughts now are with the two injured officers."

<p>
&#149;11.30pm
David Blunkett hints Mr Blair will carry out his
 cabinet reshuffle tomorrow, saying: "Time will tell tomorrow,
 whether I'm at the Home Office." 

<p>&#149;11.25pmSunderland North Labour - third hold: 5% for "metric
 martyr" independent candidate. Turnout down 10%.

<p>
&#149;11.23pmHamilton SouthLabour holds second seat - but turnout
 down 14%.

<p>
&#149;11.05pm
Prime minister's official spokesman, Alastair
 Campbell, says: "We are going to win, and win with a big majority.
 Labour will lose some seats and some good people due to tactical
 voting."

<p>&#149;10.45pmSunderland South Labour hold: Chris Mullin MP. Turnout
 48.3%, down 10% on 1997. Mr Mullin says: "One nation politics
 has again triumphed over two nation politics." 

<p>
&#149;10.05pm The deputy prime minister, John Prescott, welcomes
 the exit poll, saying: "We've done a good job so far. But there's
 stil a lot more to do and if we've convinced the electorate
 to give us a chance we would be delighted to get on with it."
 

<p>
&#149;10.03pm The shadow chancellor, Michael Portillo, says:
 "I hope very much that William Hague stays as leader. It was
 the best campaign."

<p>&#149; Two RUC officer wounded in shooting outside a polling
 station in Co Londonderry. The incident took place outside a
 polling station in Draperstown as the general election in Northern
 Ireland drew to a close.

<p>It is understood a gunman stepped out of a car near a primary
 school where voting was taking place and fired shots. One victim
 sustained a shoulder injury and another was struck in the arm.
